Equity Market Reactions to Corporate Debt IPOs Job Market Paper

Bayesian event-study of stock reactions to a firm’s first public debt offering

Paper PDF

Abstract. This paper studies how equity markets react to a firm’s first public debt offering. I use a Bayesian econometrics framework to estimate abnormal returns and their uncertainty while incorporating macro controls and firm characteristics. I find a significant negative announcement effect: cumulative abnormal returns are about −0.5% to −8% in short windows. Losses deepen over the following month and remain robust after controlling for market movements and volatility. The evidence is consistent with investors interpreting initial public debt offerings as signals of higher leverage or weaker growth prospects. The contribution is a flexible Bayesian approach that quantifies uncertainty and includes relevant firm and market factors.

Corporate Debt IPO and Firm Growth: A Random Forest Approach with Sectoral Analysis Working Paper

Evidence from 1.5 million U.S. firm-year records, 1970–2024

Paper PDF

Abstract. Using a dataset of more than 1.5 million U.S. firm records from 1970 to 2024, this study evaluates how corporate debt initial public offerings affect firm asset growth. I apply Random Forest analysis, using the principal amount raised at the debt IPO as the main treatment variable, and examine outcomes across financial and non-financial sectors. The findings indicate a significant positive association between debt IPO size and subsequent asset growth among non-financial firms.
